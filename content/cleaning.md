As you may know, it is crucial to not use any heat on faux fur when washing it. Faux fur is made of small plastic strands, and harsh chemicals and high temperatures will melt and destroy the fur fibers. It is therefore important to treat your fursuits with proper care.

You can wash fursuit parts *except the head* in a washing machine using a cold, gentle setting. For fursuit parts *except the head* you can also soak them in your bathtub. For these types of washes, regular detergent will work fine. I can recommend Blenda Sensitive. I would advise against using Klar vaskemiddel. Do not use any detergent made for wool. Dry cleaning and other similar products may destroy your fur. Therefore, I recommend doing some research before cleaning your fursuit with heavy cleaning products. You could for example test the detergent on a fur sample (ask me for one if needed) to ensure the product is safe for your fur.

When you're putting the body, tail, and paws in the washing machine (do not put the head in the washing machine), I recommend putting smaller parts such as the paws in a cleaning bag. Use a gentle setting and cold water (30 °C max if your machine doesn't have a cold setting, but check your machine instructions). You can remove the stuffing from most tails, except if you have a smaller tail with no zipper opening.

If your fursuit uses mesh eyes that are not sublimated, take care to not get the eyes wet, as the paint on the mesh may be water-soluble. Heads with sublimated eyes can be soaked in the tub as well. I recommend cleaning your fursuit head in the bathtub or wipe it down with a damp cloth. Be careful and talk to the maker about how the head should preferably be cleaned. If the eyes are not waterproof, this is especially important to be mindful of. To reduce bacterial growth inside the suit, it is highly recommended you use a fursuit spray and spray the inside of your head after every wear. It is also recommended wearing a balaclava inside your suit (contact me about getting one for free).

When drying, let the fursuit paws hang down from a clothing rack or the like. Avoid having the fur be bent or lay in an unnatural position, as the fur may notdry in the correct position. Feetpaws and handpaws may take longer to dry since they are stuffed.n Make sure that the suit is completely dry before putting it away. You can use a fan to dry the bodysuit as long as you make sure it is not too hot and not too close to the suit. When drying your suit, do not put it in the dryer. There is a risk you may accidentally put it on a hot setting or that the machine malfunctions and melts the fur. It may also lead the fur to dry in a different direction, making the fur look ruffled and unclean. It is therefore recommended to air dry it and brush it on regular intervals (every few hours or so) to ensure the fur dries in the correct position.
